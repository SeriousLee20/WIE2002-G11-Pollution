Definition

Land pollution is a serious issue. Soil contamination is a genuine problem that includes both governmental forces and citizens because of its direct influence on the environment and, as a result, on the health of humans, animals, and plants.

Soil

Issue and concern

Soil is a finite resource that cannot be replenished. It can degrade quickly (in a few years or decades), yet it takes thousands of years to develop and rejuvenate.

Factors

Urbanization and Construction

While urbanization is not in itself littering, large quantities of people living, producing trash and littering in a dense area does inevitably lead to land pollution.

Mining

Mining is the extraction of minerals and other geological materials from the ground, which are then used for a wide range of purposes, including but not limited to, producing gasoline for automobiles, generating electricity, and selling materials such as gold and silver.

Agriculture

Agriculture is foundational for both everyday life, as well as the economy as a whole. It also, however, can have profound effects on the planet. Agricultural pollution occurs when contamination created as a by-product of raising livestock and growing food crops is released into the environment, and the contamination is vast.
