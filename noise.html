Definition

What does it mean?

Noise pollution is any undesired or irritating sound that has an adverse effect on the health and well-being of people and other species is referred to as noise pollution.

Introduction

When sound reached 85 decibels (dB) or higher.

Sound travels faster in water. Therefore, the harm of noise on marine animals is much more significant.

The noise of power lawn movers can reach 90dB.

The noise of loud rock concert can reach 110dB-120dB.

Underwater Reference Station

Currently, there are 12 underwater reference stations in U.S. waters which track ocean noise levels. This effort leads to another two initiatives as well.

  1. Producing maps of where these animals are located and in what densities.
  2. Predicting levels of underwater noise from human activities.

Quieting Designs

The design of silent propeller is proposed to benefit both the marine lives and business.

Sound Proofing Designs

Design the building with sound proofing structure or materials if it is expected to encounter huge noise frequently.

Noise Cancelling Earphones

To add on the sound proofing building, noise cancelling earphones help blocking the noise to a higher extent.
